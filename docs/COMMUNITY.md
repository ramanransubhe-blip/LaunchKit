# Community

Welcome to the DevLaunchKit community! We are builders, learners, and collaborators working together to create the best open-source SaaS starter kit. This guide covers how our community is organized, how to participate, and how to get recognized for your contributions.

---

## Discord Server

Our Discord server is the heartbeat of the DevLaunchKit community. Join us at **[discord.gg/devlaunchkit](https://discord.gg/devlaunchkit)**.

### Channel Structure

| Channel            | Purpose                                                                  | Who Should Use It                   |
| :----------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------- |
| **#announcements** | Official project announcements, releases, and important updates          | Maintainers post; everyone reads    |
| **#general**       | Casual conversation, introductions, and community bonding                | Everyone                            |
| **#help**          | Quick questions about setup, configuration, and usage                    | Anyone needing real-time assistance |
| **#contributing**  | Discussion about contributing, PR help, and finding good first issues    | New and active contributors         |
| **#architecture**  | Deep technical discussions about design patterns and system architecture | Contributors and maintainers        |
| **#showcase**      | Share what you have built with DevLaunchKit                              | Everyone                            |
| **#feedback**      | Product feedback, UX suggestions, and feature brainstorming              | Everyone                            |
| **#jobs**          | Job postings from companies using DevLaunchKit (must be relevant)        | Employers and job seekers           |
| **#off-topic**     | Non-project conversation, memes, and general banter                      | Everyone                            |
| **#maintainers**   | Private channel for core team coordination                               | Maintainers only                    |
| **#triage**        | Issue triage coordination and on-call handoffs                           | Maintainers and Committers          |

### Discord Rules

1. **Be respectful** — Follow the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) in all interactions
2. **Use the right channel** — Keep conversations on-topic for each channel
3. **No spam or self-promotion** — Share your work in `#showcase`, not in every channel
4. **Search before asking** — Check pinned messages and recent history before posting questions
5. **Help others** — If you know the answer, share it; we all grow together

## Community Meetups

We organize virtual and in-person meetups for deeper engagement.

### Virtual Meetups

- **Monthly Community Call** — First Wednesday of each month at 5:00 PM UTC. A 45-minute session covering project updates, contributor spotlights, and a Q&A segment. Join via the Discord `#events` voice channel.
- **Quarterly Roadmap Review** — A live session where the core team presents the upcoming quarter's priorities and collects community feedback. Dates announced in `#announcements`.

### In-Person Meetups

We encourage community members to organize local meetups. If you are interested in hosting a DevLaunchKit meetup in your city:

1. Post in `#general` on Discord to gauge interest
2. Coordinate with the community team at [hello@devlaunchkit.dev](mailto:hello@devlaunchkit.dev)
3. We can provide presentation templates, stickers, and promotional support

### Conference Talks

If you are giving a talk about DevLaunchKit at a conference, let us know! We will:

- Amplify your talk on our social channels
- Provide official slide templates and graphics
- Feature your talk in our community showcase

## Contributor Recognition Program

We believe every contribution matters. Here is how we recognize community members:

### Contribution Types We Celebrate

| Contribution      | Examples                                                 |
| :---------------- | :------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Code**          | Bug fixes, features, performance improvements, refactors |
| **Documentation** | Guides, tutorials, API docs, translations                |
| **Community**     | Answering questions, mentoring, organizing meetups       |
| **Design**        | UI/UX improvements, icons, branding assets               |
| **Testing**       | Bug reports, test coverage, QA feedback                  |
| **Content**       | Blog posts, videos, conference talks about DevLaunchKit  |

### Recognition Tiers

#### 🌱 First-Time Contributor

- **Criteria:** First merged PR or accepted contribution
- **Recognition:** Welcome message from a maintainer, added to the Contributors list in the README
- **Reward:** DevLaunchKit contributor badge for your GitHub profile

#### ⭐ Regular Contributor

- **Criteria:** 5 or more merged PRs or sustained community participation over 2 months
- **Recognition:** Shoutout in the monthly community call and release notes
- **Reward:** Invitation to the private `#contributors` Discord channel

#### 🏆 Outstanding Contributor

- **Criteria:** 15 or more significant contributions, or leadership in a major initiative
- **Recognition:** Featured in the project README, spotlight blog post on the website
- **Reward:** DevLaunchKit swag pack (t-shirt, stickers, and laptop decal)

#### 💎 Core Contributor

- **Criteria:** Sustained, high-impact contributions over 6+ months; nominated by a Maintainer
- **Recognition:** Listed on the project website team page, permanent credit in the repository
- **Reward:** Considered for the Committer role (see [Governance](./GOVERNANCE.md))

### Monthly Contributor Spotlight

Each month, we highlight one community member who has made exceptional contributions. The spotlight includes:

- A feature in the `#announcements` Discord channel
- A shoutout in the release notes
- A profile on the DevLaunchKit website

Nominations are welcome — tag a maintainer in `#general` to nominate someone.

## Ambassador Program

DevLaunchKit Ambassadors are community leaders who represent the project in their local developer communities.

### What Ambassadors Do

- Organize local meetups and workshops focused on DevLaunchKit
- Create educational content (blog posts, videos, tutorials)
- Represent DevLaunchKit at conferences and developer events
- Provide feedback from their communities to the core team
- Help onboard new contributors in their region or language

### Ambassador Benefits

- **Early access** to upcoming features and release candidates
- **Direct communication channel** with the core team
- **Speaking opportunities** at DevLaunchKit official events
- **Swag and promotional materials** for events and meetups
- **Co-branded content** featured on official DevLaunchKit channels
- **Reference letter** from the project for professional use

### How to Become an Ambassador

1. **Demonstrate community involvement** — Active in Discord, authored content, or organized events
2. **Apply** — Send an email to [hello@devlaunchkit.dev](mailto:hello@devlaunchkit.dev) with:
   - Your name and location
   - Links to your community activity (talks, posts, meetups)
   - A brief description of how you plan to represent DevLaunchKit
3. **Interview** — A short conversation with a core team member to discuss goals and alignment
4. **Onboarding** — Access to the Ambassador toolkit, brand assets, and private coordination channel

Ambassadors are reviewed annually. Inactive ambassadors are moved to alumni status with gratitude.

## Social Media Guidelines

When representing DevLaunchKit on social media, please follow these guidelines:

### Official Accounts

- **GitHub:** [github.com/devlaunchkit](https://github.com/devlaunchkit)
- **Twitter/X:** @devlaunchkit
- **Website:** [devlaunchkit.dev](https://devlaunchkit.dev)

### For Community Members

**Do:**

- Share your experiences building with DevLaunchKit
- Use the hashtag **#DevLaunchKit** so we can find and amplify your posts
- Tag the official account when sharing projects, tutorials, or feedback
- Be authentic — speak from your personal experience, not as a project spokesperson

**Don't:**

- Imply official endorsement unless you are a Maintainer or Ambassador
- Share confidential information from private channels (security reports, unreleased features)
- Engage in heated arguments on behalf of the project — disengage and let the core team handle it
- Use the DevLaunchKit logo or branding in ways that imply ownership or official partnership without permission

### Content We Love to Amplify

- "I built X with DevLaunchKit" posts with screenshots or demos
- Tutorials and how-to guides for specific use cases
- Honest comparisons and reviews (even critical ones — we learn from feedback)
- Conference talks and workshop recordings
- Open-source projects that extend DevLaunchKit

Tag us or post in `#showcase` on Discord, and we will share your work with the community.

## Getting Started

Ready to get involved? Here are your first steps:

1. ⭐ **Star the repository** on GitHub to show your support
2. 💬 **Join our Discord** at [discord.gg/devlaunchkit](https://discord.gg/devlaunchkit) and introduce yourself in `#general`
3. 📖 **Read the docs** — Start with the [Quick Start Guide](./QUICK_START.md) and [Contributing Guide](../CONTRIBUTING.md)
4. 🔍 **Find an issue** — Look for issues labeled [`good first issue`](https://github.com/devlaunchkit/devlaunchkit/labels/good%20first%20issue) or [`help wanted`](https://github.com/devlaunchkit/devlaunchkit/labels/help%20wanted)
5. 🛠️ **Submit your first PR** — Follow the [Contributing Guide](../CONTRIBUTING.md) to get started
6. 🎉 **Celebrate** — Every contribution counts, no matter how small

We are excited to have you here. Welcome to DevLaunchKit!
